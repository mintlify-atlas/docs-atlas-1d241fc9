Wordle LOTR is a Lord of the Rings themed word-guessing game built for Android and iOS with React Native and Expo. Players are challenged to identify Middle-earth words — from character names to places and legendary artifacts — using color-coded letter feedback and two lore-based hints per round. Whether you're a seasoned Tolkien reader or a fan of the films, Wordle LOTR puts your knowledge of Middle-earth to the test one word at a time.

## Key Features

## How It Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="A random Middle-earth word is selected">
    When a new round begins, the app picks a word at random from the Middle-earth dictionary. The word length varies, and the grid updates automatically to match it.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Type a guess and get color-coded feedback">
    Enter a word of the same length and tap **Aceptar** to submit. Each letter tile changes color — green for a correct letter in the right spot, yellow for a correct letter in the wrong spot, and grey for a letter that isn't in the word at all.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use lore hints to narrow down the answer">
    Two hints pulled from the word's lore entry are always visible on screen. If you've made three or more attempts without success, a button appears letting you reveal the answer outright and move on.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Tech Stack

Wordle LOTR is built on **React Native 0.81** and **Expo SDK 54**, using **expo-router** for file-based navigation. The codebase is written in **TypeScript** throughout. Animations are handled by **react-native-reanimated**, and a confetti burst powered by **react-native-confetti-cannon** fires off whenever you guess correctly. The project also leverages **expo-haptics** for tactile feedback and **@expo/vector-icons** for UI iconography.
